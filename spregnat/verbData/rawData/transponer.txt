USAGE NOTE
This word may also be spelled "transponer."
trasponer
TRANSITIVE VERB
1. (to switch) 
a. to transpose 
Traspuse las últimas dos columnas para que los datos fueran más claros.I transposed the last two columns so that the data were clearer.
b. to transplant (plant) 
Le pedí al jardinero que traspusiera las margaritas a un lugar donde les diera el sol.I asked the gardener to transplant the daisies where they could get more sun.
2. (to hide) 
a. to disappear 
Dos ovejas traspusieron el monte y el pastor no las encontraba.Two sheep disappeared behind the hill, and the shepherd couldn't find them.
3. (to go beyond) 
a. to get past 
Mis amigos me retaron a trasponer la puerta de la casa abandonada.My friends challenged me to get past the door of the abandoned house.
trasponerse
PRONOMINAL VERB
4. (to switch places) 
a. to change 
En el experimento vimos como un líquido se traspuso en un sólido.In the experiment, we saw how a liquid changed into a solid.
5. (to hide) 
a. to disappear 
Al ver al animal salvaje, Miguel se traspuso detrás de un árbol.When he saw the wild animal, Miguel disappeared behind a tree.
b. to set (sun) 
Esperamos en la playa hasta que se traspusiera el sol.We stayed at the beach, waiting for the sun to set.
6. (to fall asleep) 
a. to doze off 
Los niños se traspusieron en el auto de regreso del colegio.The kids dozed off in the car on the way back from school.
